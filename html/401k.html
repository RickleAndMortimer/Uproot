401(k) - What Even Is That?

Description:

401(k) plan is an employer-sponsored defined-contribution pension account defined in subsection 401(k) of the Internal Revenue Code. 401k allows your employer to match the money you put in to invest for when you retire.

  • A plan that is a company-sponsored retirement account.

  • Tax-free after retirement.

  • Employers can match the contribution made by employees.

  • Employees' maximum contribution is $19,500 per year under 50 and $26,000 over the age of 50.

  • Employers' maximum match contribution is $58,000 or 100% of employee salary per year, whichever is lower, applies to overages of 50 of $64,500.
