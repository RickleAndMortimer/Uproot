College Financial Package Lingo

Cost of Attendance (COA):

The full cost of attending the college including tuition, fees, textbooks, and room and board if applicable.

Financial Applications:

Requires family financial information such as tax information. Should have everything prepared when filing out these applications to make it an easy process.

FAFSA (Free Application for Federal Student Aid):

state and federal grants, loans, and work-study.

EFC (Estimated Family Contribution):

Estimated cost that family would have to pay.

CSS (College Scholarship Service profile):

Not required by all colleges but mostly private schools, check college requirements if applicable.

529 Plan:

Parents put money in a savings account for a child's college COA.

NPC (Net Price Calculator):

COA estimator

Gift Aid:

money that you do not have to pay back.

Grants

Scholarships

Work-Study:

Working part-time on campus operated by colleges, where the salary will be paid by the federal government but goes directly to the colleges to deduct from COA.

Student Loans:

Must repay money

Subsidized Loans

Repay without interest

Unsubsidized Loans

Must repay with interest
