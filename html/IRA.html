IRA - Individual Retirement Account

IRA:

A brokerage account that has tax-advantages where you contribute money into individual stocks, ETFs, mutual funds, or index funds.

Maximum of $6,000 per year contribution in IRA or $7,000 if 50 or older.

Withdrawals before age 59.5 trigger capital gains tax + 10% IRA penalty.

Withdrawals of contributions do not receive any penalty.

Two types:

Traditional IRA

Expect more income / taxes to be less in retirement than currently.

Funds are taxed when withdrawn

2021 Traditional IRA Income and Contribution Limits

source: irs.gov

Roth IRA

Expect income / taxes to be more in retirement than currently.

Contributions are not taxed when withdrawn if requirements are met.

For most students who have less income and would pay more taxes in the future, it should be more reasonable to open a Roth IRA.
